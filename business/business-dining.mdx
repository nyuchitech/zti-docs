## Business Dining Etiquette in Zimbabwe

<Card title="Cultural Tips" icon="handshake">

**Before the Meal:**
- Host typically pays (clarify in advance if splitting)
- Arrive on time or slightly early
- Greetings are important - take time for pleasantries
- Business cards still commonly exchanged

**During the Meal:**
- Let host or most senior person lead ordering
- Pace yourself to match others
- Business discussion typically comes after ordering/first course
- Avoid controversial political topics

**Payment:**
- The person who invited typically pays
- Tipping: 10-15% if service not included
- Credit cards accepted at business-level restaurants
- Have USD cash as backup

**Dress Code:**
- When in doubt, dress more formally
- Men: Collared shirt, long trousers minimum
- Women: Business casual to professional
- Safari/casual wear not appropriate for business meals in cities
</Card>

## Booking Tips

<Card title="Making Reservations" icon="calendar">

**What to Specify:**
- Number of guests
- Time required
- "Business meeting" or "client entertainment"
- Quiet table request
- Any dietary requirements
- Payment arrangements (if corporate account)

**When to Book:**
- Fine dining: 1-2 days minimum, week+ for weekends
- Business casual: Same day often fine, but call ahead
- Groups 6+: Always book in advance
- Private dining: 1-2 weeks notice

**Confirming Details:**
- Reconfirm morning of reservation
- Provide mobile number
- Arrive 5-10 minutes early
- Have backup restaurant in mind
</Card>
