# Emergency Contacts Directory

Essential emergency and assistance contacts for travelers in Zimbabwe. Save this page to your phone before traveling.

<Warning>
**In a Life-Threatening Emergency:** Call **995** (Police) or **994** (Medical) immediately.
</Warning>

### Reporting Incidents

<Card>
**What to Report:**
- Theft or robbery
- Lost passport (also notify embassy)
- Traffic accidents
- Assault or crimes

**How to Report:**
1. Call 995 for emergencies
2. Visit nearest police station for non-emergencies
3. Request a police report number (needed for insurance)
4. Get copies of all documentation

**Important:** For insurance claims, you will need a police report with case number.
</Card>

- Zimbabwe's primary air ambulance

- 24-hour emergency response

- Regional air evacuation service
- Kenya HQ: +254 20 699 2000
- 24-hour: +254 20 699 2299

**Netcare 911 (South Africa)**
- Phone: +27 82 911
- Cross-border evacuation to SA

**Your Travel Insurance:**
- Keep policy details accessible
- Note emergency contact number
- Understand what's covered before you need it
</Card>

<Note>
**Important:** Most travel insurance policies have a 24-hour emergency line. Save this number in your phone before traveling. Medical evacuation from Zimbabwe to Johannesburg or Harare can cost $15,000-30,000+ without insurance.
</Note>

## Lost Documents

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Lost Passport">
    **Immediate Steps:**
    1. Report to nearest police station
    2. Get police report with case number
    3. Contact your embassy immediately
    4. Embassy will issue emergency travel document
    5. May take 1-3 business days

    **What You'll Need:**
    - Police report
    - Passport photos (2)
    - Proof of citizenship if available
    - Travel itinerary
    - Fee payment (varies by country)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lost Credit/Debit Cards">
    **Immediate Steps:**
    1. Call your bank's international emergency line
    2. Block/cancel the card immediately
    3. Request emergency replacement if available
    4. File police report if stolen

    **Emergency Card Numbers:**
    - Visa Global: +1 303 967 1096
    - Mastercard Global: +1 636 722 7111
    - Amex Global: +1 336 393 1111
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lost Travel Insurance Documents">
    - Contact your insurance provider's emergency line
    - Policy numbers can usually be looked up with your name/DOB
    - Keep digital copies in email or cloud storage
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Safari & Wildlife Emergencies

<Card title="Safari Emergency Procedures" icon="paw">

**If You Encounter Wildlife Danger:**
1. Stay calm - don't run
2. Follow your guide's instructions exactly
3. Back away slowly if alone
4. Make yourself appear large (elephants)
5. Do not make direct eye contact with predators

**Safari Lodge Emergencies:**
- All lodges have emergency protocols
- Radio communication to rangers
- Medical kits on safari vehicles
- Evacuation plans in place

**Snakebite:**
1. Keep calm and immobilize the limb
2. DO NOT try to suck out venom
3. Get to medical facility immediately
4. Try to identify/photograph snake if safe

**Medical Kits:**
- All reputable safari operators carry first aid
- Anti-venom typically not carried (hospital treatment)
- Evacuation to hospital is standard protocol
</Card>
