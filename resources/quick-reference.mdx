## Common Guest Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is it safe to walk around Victoria Falls town?">
    Yes, Victoria Falls town is very safe for tourists during the day. At night, stick to main roads and use taxis between venues. The tourist police patrol regularly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can guests see the falls from Zambia side too?">
    Yes! The Zimbabwe side has the majority (70%) of views, but Zambia offers different perspectives. Day trips to Zambia are easy - just walk across the bridge. Need passport and may need visa.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How far in advance should activities be booked?">
    Popular activities (helicopter flights, rafting) should be booked 1-2 days ahead in high season (Jul-Oct). Same-day booking often possible in low season.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the best time to see the falls?">
    Morning (before 10am) has best lighting for photos and fewer crowds. Afternoon can have rainbow in the spray. Full moon nights offer "lunar rainbow" experience (special tours available).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are there ATMs that work for international cards?">
    Yes, but reliability varies. Standard Chartered and FBC Bank ATMs are most reliable for international cards. Always advise guests to have USD cash backup.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do guests tip for safari?">
    Guidelines: $10-20 per person per day for guide, $5-10 per person per day for lodge staff. Tips in USD cash, given directly or in staff tip box.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can guests visit both Zimbabwe and Zambia?">
    Yes! Options:
    - **KAZA UniVisa** ($50): Allows multiple crossings for 30 days
    - **Day Visa**: Single entry to other country
    - Walk across Victoria Falls Bridge (10 min crossing)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is the local water safe to drink?">
    No - always recommend bottled water. All hotels provide bottled water. Use bottled water for brushing teeth in rural areas.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
