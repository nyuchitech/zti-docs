## Why Sell Zimbabwe?

<Card>
**Key Selling Points for Your Clients:**

- **Victoria Falls:** One of the Seven Natural Wonders - a bucket-list destination
- **Exceptional Safari:** Hwange, Mana Pools - intimate wildlife experiences without the crowds
- **Value:** Excellent quality across all budget levels
- **Authenticity:** Less commercialized than other African destinations
- **Accessibility:** English-speaking, USD currency, stable tourism infrastructure
- **Combination Trips:** Easy to combine with Botswana, Zambia, South Africa

**Market Positioning:**
- Premium safari seekers looking beyond Kenya/Tanzania
- Adventure travelers wanting more than just game drives
- Cultural explorers interested in Great Zimbabwe and local heritage
- Honeymooners seeking romantic, exclusive experiences
</Card>

## FAM Trip Opportunities {#fam-trips}

<Card title="Familiarization Tours" icon="plane">

**Why Do a Zimbabwe FAM?**
- Experience the product first-hand
- Build relationships with local operators
- Create compelling sales content
- Understand logistics and client expectations

**How to Access FAM Trips:**

1. **Through Consortia:** Major consortia (Virtuoso, Signature, etc.) organize Zimbabwe FAMs
2. **Operator FAMs:** Many DMCs offer agent FAMs - contact directly
3. **ZTA Trade Events:** Zimbabwe Tourism Authority hosts periodic trade visits
4. **Self-FAM:** Negotiate reduced rates with operators for site inspections

**Typical FAM Inclusions:**
- Accommodation at partner properties
- Activities and experiences
- Transfers and logistics
- Meals (often full board)
- Site inspections at multiple properties

**Agent Qualifications:**
- Active travel agent credentials
- Demonstrated Zimbabwe selling potential
- Social media/marketing commitment
- Often require minimum booking commitment

</Card>

## Trade Support & Resources {#trade-support}

### Accessing Trade Rates

<Card>
**How Agent/Trade Rates Work:**

Most Zimbabwe operators offer trade rates (typically 10-20% commission or net rates):

1. **Register with Operators:** Complete trade registration with each supplier
2. **Provide Credentials:** IATA/ASTA number, business registration, references
3. **Access Rates:** Via agent portals, rate sheets, or direct quotes
4. **Terms Vary:** Each operator has different policies

**Standard Requirements:**
- Valid travel agent license/registration
- Proof of bonding/insurance
- Business registration documents
- Minimum booking volumes (sometimes)

**Tips:**
- Build relationships with key DMCs
- Attend trade shows to establish connections
- Consider a receptive operator for one-stop shopping
- Ask about override commissions for volume
</Card>

## Booking & Reservations

### How to Book

<Card>
**Booking Channels:**

1. **Direct with Operators:**
   - Email reservations teams
   - Use agent portals where available
   - Phone for complex requests

2. **Through DMC/Receptive:**
   - One contact for complete packages
   - Local expertise and support
   - Often better rates for multi-component bookings

3. **Online Booking Systems:**
   - Some operators on Tourplan, Wetu, etc.
   - Real-time availability where available

**Standard Booking Flow:**
1. Request quote with client requirements
2. Receive proposal and rates
3. Confirm booking with deposit
4. Provide rooming list and flight details
5. Final payment per terms
6. Receive final documentation

</Card>

### Payment Terms

<Card>
**Typical Payment Terms:**

| Component | Deposit | Final Payment |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Safari Lodges | 20-50% | 30-60 days before |
| Hotels | First night or 10% | 14-30 days before |
| Activities | Sometimes prepaid | On confirmation |
| Transfers | Varies | With accommodation |

**Payment Methods:**
- Bank transfer (most common for trade)
- Credit card (higher fees, some accept)
- Trade credit (established accounts)

**Currency:**
- Most rates quoted in USD
- Some operators quote per person sharing
- Clarify single supplement, child rates

**Cancellation:**
- Varies by operator and season
- Peak season: stricter terms
- Safari lodges: typically stricter than hotels
- Always clarify before confirming
</Card>

## Selling Tips & Client FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to Position Zimbabwe vs. Competitors">
    **Against Kenya/Tanzania:**
    - More intimate, less crowded safari experience
    - Walking safaris and canoe safaris (unique)
    - Better value at luxury level
    - Victoria Falls as anchor attraction

    **Against Botswana:**
    - More affordable (similar quality)
    - More variety (falls, ruins, mountains)
    - Easier combination options

    **Against South Africa:**
    - More authentic safari experience
    - Victoria Falls vs. no equivalent
    - Less commercial/tourist-developed
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Common Client Concerns & Responses">
    **"Is Zimbabwe safe?"**
    Zimbabwe has been politically stable for tourism, and tourist areas are well-managed and secure. Victoria Falls and safari areas have excellent safety records.

    **"What about the economy/currency?"**
    Tourists use US Dollars - the local currency situation doesn't affect travelers. Major hotels and lodges operate to international standards.

    **"Is it expensive?"**
    Zimbabwe offers excellent value across all levels. Mid-range options are particularly good value compared to Botswana and East Africa.

    **"What about medical facilities?"**
    Private clinics in major areas, evacuation coverage included in good travel insurance. Victoria Falls has reliable medical services.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Best Seller Itineraries">
    **The Classic (7-10 days):**
    Victoria Falls (3 nights) → Hwange (3 nights) → Optional extension

    **Victoria Falls + Botswana (7 days):**
    Victoria Falls (3 nights) → Chobe (2-3 nights)

    **Safari Circuit (10-14 days):**
    Victoria Falls → Hwange → Mana Pools → Harare

    **History & Culture (7 days):**
    Harare → Great Zimbabwe → Eastern Highlands → Victoria Falls
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Seasonal Selling Calendar">
    **Peak (July-October):** Best wildlife, highest demand, book early
    **Shoulder (May-June, November):** Great value, good conditions
    **Green (December-April):** Low rates, lush scenery, Victoria Falls peak flow

    **Key Booking Windows:**
    - Peak season: 6-12 months ahead
    - Shoulder: 3-6 months ahead
    - Green season: Shorter lead times possible
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
