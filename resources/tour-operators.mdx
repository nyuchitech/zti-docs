## Booking Tips

<Card title="How to Choose an Operator" icon="check">

**Red Flags:**
- No physical address or unclear location
- Pressure to pay in unusual ways
- Unable to provide references
- No reviews or very new operation
- Significantly cheaper than competitors

**Good Signs:**
- Member of tourism associations (ZTA, SATOA)
- Established web presence
- Verifiable reviews on TripAdvisor, Google
- Clear terms and conditions
- Responsive communication
- Proper insurance and safety equipment

**Before Booking:**
1. Research reviews on multiple platforms
2. Ask for references (especially for expensive bookings)
3. Confirm all inclusions in writing
4. Understand cancellation policies
5. Use secure payment methods
6. Get emergency contact numbers
</Card>

### Questions to Ask

<Card>
**For Safari Lodges:**
- What's the guide-to-guest ratio?
- What vehicles do you use?
- What activities are included?
- What's the typical wildlife viewing?
- Are there age restrictions?

**For Activities:**
- What safety equipment is provided?
- What's your safety record?
- Are guides certified?
- What happens if weather cancels?
- What's included (photos, refreshments)?

**For Transfers:**
- What type of vehicle?
- Is it air-conditioned?
- How long is the journey?
- Are stops included?
- What if flight is delayed?
</Card>

## Tourism Associations

<Card title="Industry Bodies" icon="building-columns">

**Zimbabwe Tourism Authority (ZTA)**
- Official tourism body
- Licensing and standards
- Web: zimbabwetourism.net

**Safari Operators Association of Zimbabwe (SOAZ)**
- Safari industry body
- Member operators follow code of conduct

**Hospitality Association of Zimbabwe (HAZ)**
- Hotel and hospitality standards

**Verify Membership:**
When in doubt, ask if an operator is registered with ZTA or relevant association. Legitimate operators are happy to provide registration details.
</Card>
